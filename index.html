About AES & DES

AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) is a symmetric encryption algorithm widely used across the globe. It encrypts data in fixed blocks of 128 bits using 128, 192, or 256-bit keys, making it highly secure and efficient.

DES (Data Encryption Standard) is an older symmetric encryption method. It uses a 56-bit key and operates on 64-bit blocks. Although less secure today, it is important historically and as a basis for understanding encryption methods.
